Lately, I've been thinking about how everything becomes tough when I try to do things out of a sense of duty. Every day, I meditate, work out (exercise), study English, read, take on small personal challenges, and keep various logs (like what I ate, my health, mood, good things, things I'm grateful for), reflect, and write a diary.

I have also set up a system where, unless I complete certain tasks, I can't do things like watch YouTube (I won't go into technical details here, but I might explain it in another post if there's interest). This works well for me since I easily give in to temptation, but the problem is that when it feels like a "duty," it becomes hard to keep up every day.

Of course, it's fine on days when I feel relaxed. But I do some reflecting at night, and when I'm busy with work during weekdays, some of my daily tasks spill over into the evening. On those days, if I'm tired from work or lacking sleep, it can be hard to get all my tasks done at night.

Even when I'm sleepy and not planning to watch more YouTube, I tell myself I need to finish my tasks before bed, which often leads to just wasting time and sleeping less in the end.

I've started to feel that staying up just to complete my daily tasks is like being hard on myself. These activities are for self-improvement and done for my own benefit, but even though doing them daily is good for forming habits, forcing myself to do them under any circumstances just seems harmful. Sleep deprivation affects my energy for the next day, creating a vicious cycle.

I think this happens because of a sense of duty. The thought that I must do something because I always do it, or shouldn't do something because I don't usually, can help build habits but also make me feel stuck.

When I reflect, most of my actions seem to be driven by this sense of duty. I was planning to write another blog post today because I felt I had to, not because I wanted to, which meant I didn't get around to writing it and kept putting it off.

The idea that I "should write today" comes from thinking that if I don’t do it today, I probably won’t do it tomorrow, either, similar to the saying "tomorrow never comes."

But deep down, I do want to write. If I didn’t, it’s not like I’d suffer any dire consequences like dying or getting arrested for not blogging. But to avoid procrastination, I tell myself I have to write today.

However, maybe it’s a "chicken or the egg" situation. Is the sense of duty causing procrastination, or is procrastination causing the sense of duty?

I feel like if I just wanted to write without overthinking, it wouldn't be a big deal. It's hard because of thoughts like "what if people think it’s weird" or "I want it to be perfect, but that's a lot of work" or "I don’t want it to be just three lines long." These thoughts might be tied to the sense of duty or procrastination.

Overall, I might just be overthinking—worrying about something that doesn’t matter. It might be better to be honest with myself. It’s okay if not everyone likes what I do, as long as I express my thoughts. There can be blog posts that are only three lines long. From now on, I want to approach things, not just writing, with this mindset.
