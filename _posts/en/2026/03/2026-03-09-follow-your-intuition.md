### Advice Isn't Always Right
Many lessons tell us to listen to others' advice. But is that really true?

For example, say you have a crush on someone and you want to confess but are scared of rejection. You decide to ask a friend for advice.

Your friend says, "That person you like is actually known to cheat and date many people. You shouldn't confess. You'll just end up hurt."

Should you listen to this advice? Your friend might just be saying this out of jealousy. If your confession succeeds, you could be very happy. Meanwhile, your friend might be struggling to find a partner. Maybe your friend is trying to stop you from getting a partner first, masking it as logical advice.

However, advice may not always lead to happiness. Your friend's view might be skewed by jealousy, or even if your crush is unfaithful, it doesn't mean you'll be unhappy. Perhaps your crush separates serious relationships from casual ones, and you might be the special one. Knowing this, you might not feel as hurt when a fling is revealed (to be clear, this isn't saying cheating is okay—just sharing an example).

### It's Not About Good or Bad
This is an extreme example, with possible ill-intent, but sometimes advice without any bad intentions might not lead you in the best direction. People might genuinely think they are helping you with their advice, but it may not make you happy.

Take pursuing a dream. Ichiro, for example, was smart as a kid. His teachers advised him to focus on studies instead of baseball. Had he listened, he might not have become a world-famous baseball player. And it's easy to see the teachers had no ill intentions.

The point isn't about right or wrong. If Ichiro had focused on academics, he might have been as happy, if not happier. What's important isn't the outcome but what you want and how you feel.

### The True Answer Is in Your Heart
When you receive advice, you might wonder whether to follow it or trust yourself. Deep down, you probably know what you should do. You might not know the advisor's intention, and it doesn't matter. What's important is what you want.

If hearing advice clicks with something inside you, embrace it. If it doesn't and leaves you feeling uneasy, it's okay to ignore it.

It doesn't mean you should disregard the person who advised you. Be grateful for their advice and follow your own heart.

Follow your intuition, especially when you're unsure.
