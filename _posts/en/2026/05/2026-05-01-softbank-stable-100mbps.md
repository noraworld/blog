**Note: The "100 Mbps" in the title refers to the internet speed in my environment. Depending on your location and home network, you may not achieve 100 Mbps. This article does not guarantee you will always get 100 Mbps by following its advice.**

### Why Is SoftBank Hikari Slow?
If you search online, opinions on SoftBank Hikari's speed seem split: "very slow" and "very fast." It's confusing, but to confuse you even more deliberately, both are correct.

However, there's a clear distinction between these two opinions. The difference is whether or not they have the **BB Unit Rental.** Those who have it say it's "fast," and those who don't say it's "slow."

BB Unit Rental is an optional service from SoftBank Hikari. By subscribing, you receive a router from SoftBank in a few days (it took four business days for me). You can connect your devices to the internet using this router.

You can also use your router, so it's possible to use the internet with SoftBank Hikari without this option. Initially, I thought my own router was enough and didn't subscribe to this option.

However, BB Unit Rental isn't just about leasing a router. By signing up, it dramatically changes your maximum transfer speed. The advertised 1 Gbps maximum download speed is for those using this option. Without it, your maximum transfer speed decreases significantly.

Another crucial element is the "IPv6 High-Speed Hybrid" option. This helps you use an alternate path through IPv6 when IPv4 is congested. This maintains speed during peak evening hours.

By subscribing to these two options, you can significantly improve your internet speed. My previously slow connection improved substantially with these two services.
