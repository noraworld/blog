### Why SoftBank Hikari is slow
When you search about SoftBank Hikari's speed online, it seems that opinions are split into "very slow" and "very fast." This can be confusing, but to add to that confusion with an enigmatic statement: both are correct.

However, there is a clear distinction between these two opinions. The difference lies in whether you have signed up for the **BB Unit Rental or not**. Those who signed up say it's "fast," while those who haven't signed up say it's "slow."

The BB Unit Rental is an optional service from SoftBank Hikari. When you subscribe to it, you'll receive a router from SoftBank in a few days (it took about four business days for me). You can then connect your devices to the internet using this router.

You can also use a commercially available router, so you don’t necessarily need this option to use the internet through SoftBank Hikari. Initially, when I subscribed to SoftBank Hikari, I thought my existing commercial router would suffice and did not apply for this option.

However, this BB Unit Rental option is not just about lending you a router. By subscribing to this service, the maximum transfer speed changes significantly. The advertised speed, with a maximum of 1 Gbps, is only applicable if you're subscribed to this service. Without it, maximum transfer speeds are considerably reduced.

Another important factor is the "IPv6 High-Speed Hybrid" option. This allows you to bypass congested routes using IPv6 when IPv4 is crowded. With this option, you can avoid significant slowdowns during peak hours.

By subscribing to these two options, you can significantly improve your connection speed. My previously slow connection improved after applying for these options.
