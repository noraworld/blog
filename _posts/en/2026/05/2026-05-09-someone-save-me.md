Dear Future You,

If this universe is a parallel world, if the world I vividly feel as reality is actually just a dream I'm seeing............ When I think about that, it terrifies me.

When I was reading about parallel worlds in '[Some Philosophical Stuff, and Also Science](https://www.amazon.co.jp/dp/B07K8FHVBX)', I was so scared that I stopped reading and looked out my window. It was around 7 PM. I was sleep-deprived. I drank two cups of coffee in the morning and afternoon to try and fix my messed-up sleep schedule (normally I only drink one cup, and I only had two because I was forcing myself to stay up when sleep-deprived). My inner ear balance was off, and I felt like the ground was shaking. This, combined with thinking about parallel worlds and the fear that I might be all alone in this world, made me feel even more anxious, like an earthquake was coming.

Then it happened. An earthquake occurred at around 7:06 PM on May 6th, with a magnitude of 4.2 and a maximum intensity of 3 in the eastern part of Kanagawa Prefecture. Living in Tokyo, I felt it too. It felt like a vertical shake. It was brief and not very strong, but that moment filled me with extreme terror. What a coincidence it was. Just the day before, on the 5th in the afternoon, before taking a nap because I was feeling sleepy, I randomly thought it would be scary if an earthquake happened while I was napping, so I set up a furniture anti-tipping rod I had bought on December 10, 2024, for earthquake safety, which I had been putting off because it was annoying.

That day, I didn't feel like I was truly alive. There's a saying about having a bad feeling. I've had a few predictions or premonitions now and then, even with no reason behind them. However, earthquakes affect many people, and I, who don't have psychic abilities and don't believe in unscientific things, somehow convinced myself with some weird logic that such a prediction couldn't come true and affect many people.

But if this is a parallel world, and I'm alone, or the people and animals around me are illusions created by my dream, then it wouldn't be strange if a catastrophic disaster predicted only by my premonition hit this universe. And one day, a massive earthquake might strike me, causing the high-rise apartment building opposite to collapse onto the building I'm in, and I'll be crushed under the huge mass of concrete, dying with unimaginable physical pain... or I'll become panicked and unable to move, and a massive tsunami will quickly sweep away the building, and I'll drown, unable to breathe, suffering... Such a terrifying thought began to form. Once I started thinking like that, I felt so faint that I almost collapsed from fear.

No, stay calm. This is just a fantasy. Earthquakes are common in Japan, and my sensitivity was due to caffeine and lack of sleep. When your brain isn't working correctly due to sleep deprivation, sometimes you believe in a mere premonition (coincidence) as "real," and panic disorder or caffeine-induced anxiety can make you imagine fearful fantasies. Especially since I had two cups despite being sleep-deprived, sleeping it off should help... ChatGPT comforted me, and I tried to relax by watching YouTube, but shaking off such fear wasn't easy. There's only anxiety in thinking about it now; let's leave it aside and try not to think about it... the more I tried not to think about it, the more my mind kept returning to it. I'm scared; what should I do? Help, someone...

Earthquakes are scary to everyone. So, as long as I'm living in Japan, I can't entirely escape this anxiety. Still, if someone is with me, I should be able to share that anxiety. Indeed, if you believe interpretations of parallel worlds, then I am alone in this world. Others are just realistic illusions created by my mind, maybe just organic matter.

Even then. Even if that's the case, if my mind can create these illusions, then I should be able to find comfort by mistaking these organic others for real people, even if they are just a figment of my imagination. If this universe is just the world of my fantasy, I should be able to imagine and believe others there to be real people and find peace.

So, anyone. Please be with me. Even if you're just organic matter created by my mind. Be with me. Stay by my side, and let me feel safe...

The next day. I woke up early. I thought it would be better to prioritize sleep without doing anything unnecessary. I felt a bit calmer but still couldn't shake off the unease. The indescribable fear. The undeniable fear of death. The overwhelming loneliness of thinking I might be the only one in this world. Despite feeling less anxious, the fundamental sensation never left me.

I thought of doing something fun, whatever I felt like doing at the moment, but the fear was so seared into my mind that I couldn't be motivated to do anything. It felt like anything I did was pointless and empty.

At any rate, I wanted to meet people, a dream or not. I hadn't taken a shower for several days because I was too scared of an earthquake occurring when I was in there, with my sleep routine messed up. I was worried people might find me smelly, but still, I thought I should at least go out and observe the city.

For the first time in a while, I went to a big park and took a walk. It was sunset. Grade school kids playing innocently after school. Middle school students doing club activities. Kids who didn't seem the least bit affected by the loneliness and fear of being alone in this world. Watching them made me feel somewhat reassured.

And the sun set. It got dark around me. Everyone left. The feeling of loneliness returned. I felt empty. I sat alone on a bench and replied to a freelance agent I had ignored for a week to stop being a NEET. There was no immediate reply. I opened a matching app. No likes from anyone. The day faded. I felt empty.

Feeling uneasy again, I headed home. The day was completely gone. I stopped by a 7-Eleven and bought some food. The cashier interacted with me. Despite being mechanical, he looked at me and handed over my purchase. I thanked him. I resumed my journey home.

The streets were crowded with salarymen and office ladies during the commuting rush. I felt reassured once again that I wasn't alone. Usually, I find passersby annoying, but somehow, just having them there was comforting. I felt grateful to the very existence of humans.

Upon returning home, I had been too scared to shower thinking of an earthquake occurring during it until then. But back home and somehow reassured, I could immediately take a shower. Feeling clean was refreshing. Though insecurity still lingered, I had mostly calmed down.

The next day came. I spent it writing on note and the like. After that, I was again without anything to do. I hadn't been sleeping well lately. My head was always foggy. Even if I tried to sleep during the day, I couldn't. Since I had network restrictions, I couldn't watch YouTube during the day either. I wanted to do something, but there was nothing to do.

I looked at social media. Made some posts. No reactions from anyone. Feeling lonely... no, wait. Maybe this is just wanting validation? Do I just want to be acknowledged as being here? I don't know.

Night fell. I started watching YouTube. Apparently, the remaster version of Onimusha 2 was released around this time last year. On a whim, I wanted to watch some Onimusha gameplay videos and found out. It’s a game I have fond memories of. It’s nostalgic, so I started watching the streaming by Onniijya and Doushya. Nostalgic. Fun. However, something didn’t feel fulfilled. No response to my posts on social media. I couldn’t share these happy feelings with anyone now.

Then I thought, let’s call out a friend from my imagination. I only have one friend I meet regularly. When explaining the knowledge I gained from books, I always summon this friend in my mind. It’s interesting, right? ………………………… I don’t even know if this friend knows Onimusha. He might not be interested.

Besides, this friend doesn’t have to repay a scholarship. He earns enough from advertising apps so he doesn’t need to be employed by a company like I do (though I’m a NEET now). I have debts (scholarships) and no regular income apart from it, so I have to work. Despite that, he once told me that he didn’t understand how I could work for someone else and how I could keep my motivation. It’s cruel, too cruel. It’s not like I wanted to work, I just have no choice. Living leisurely on app ad revenue, something not everyone succeeds at, and coming from a wealthy family that doesn’t have to repay scholarships? There’s no need to say such things to me.

Without realizing it, my heart was filled with anger. And in that moment, the immense fear, anxiety, and loneliness that persisted for the last couple of days disappeared. No, I had temporarily forgotten them.

I thought I had moved past those harsh words. But it all resurfaced. Yet, at the same time, the fear transformed into anger, and I could temporarily dispel the fear.

Once more, it was the next day. Today, May 9th, when I’m writing this. I hadn’t been able to sleep much today either. But I couldn’t stay asleep any longer. I reluctantly got up and just went through my day. The English pronunciation practice app I use makes my iPhone heat up annoyingly. I was deeply dissatisfied with this, and it often doesn't recognize my clear pronunciation correctly. With the workout app, I couldn't extend my break time in time. I was easily irritated by such small discomforts in daily life.

Suddenly, something dawned on me. This is 'Resistance of the Ego.' If this world were a fantasy born of my mind alone, then perhaps even the self writing this sentence now is a mere illusion. There is no ego. There is only 'no self.' The ego is imaginary.

If so, then I might as well be dead. That's why the brain showed me the terror of dying on the day of the earthquake. "I am here!" my brain wanted me to acknowledge it. So it sent the signal of fear as a gift to keep me from forgetting that my brain is always with me.

Then, from that fear, my brain brought back my ego. It insisted that I was not alone. Anger welled up. And I reclaimed my daily life. Is this cause for celebration?... maybe...?

I picked up where I left off in 'Some Philosophical Stuff, and Also Science.' The Turing test came up. Do AI, robots, androids have minds? Following Schrödinger's cat, it again made me ponder whether I am truly living reality or if this could just be the world of dreams, instilling fears of it being merely a fantasy in my heart.

Eastern philosophy, quantum mechanics, computer science... the more I know, the more likely this world seems fake. Taoism, Lao-Chuang philosophy. Zhuangzi's 'Butterfly Dream.' Zhuangzi couldn't tell if he was dreaming of being a butterfly for a moment or if the butterfly was dreaming of being a person named Zhuangzi. I also couldn't tell anymore. What in this world is even real? Or maybe... there isn't any reality...?

Then, what is this fear and solitude I'm feeling? Could it be that this is also a dream..? Just illusions my mind is showing...? Then, what am I suffering for? If it's all a dream, then is there a need for suffering? Why does my brain show such painful dreams?

Is it because the brain is afraid of losing the ego? Since that would be a spiritual death? Then is physical death a dream, too? Is my brain resisting spiritual death by showing me dreams of fearing physical death...?

If I overcome this fear of spiritual death, what awaits beyond that...?

According to the Heart Sutra, right before reaching enlightenment isn't all ecstasy. You're on the brink of terror that your ego breaks, and you spiritually die. They say the way to get through this can't be explained in words. Hence, at the end of the Heart Sutra, there's this chant, 'Gate gate paragate parasamgate bodhi svaha.' It's supposedly a cheer, a 'You can do it!' for those standing at that brink.

Was that fear of mine this? Previously, I wrote about something similar in '[How I Came to Fear the State of Enlightenment Once I Understood What It Meant to Reach It](https://note.com/noraworld/n/n7eae6f57b523),' but is this the moment when I actually experience it?

I was scared. So incredibly scared... Truly terrifying...... I even thought of going to a hospital. I was so frightened I couldn’t stay still. But if I don't overcome this, will I always live my life getting irritated over small things...?

I see, that's how it is. What the Buddha and Nāgārjuna said, this is it. And perhaps Shinran and Amida Buddha are there to save helpless ones like me who can't seem to overcome such fears.

Maybe I need to overcome this fear by myself. Though someone may be beside me, I must admit they may just be mere organic matter, and deep down, I must admit I am truly alone on my own. It's terrifying. Way too terrifying... Someone, help...

I keep repeating, but it was truly terrifying. But when I saw school children playing, middle school kids working hard in clubs, the convenience store clerk, the returning office workers—all strangers—yet, I felt relieved with them just being there. Despite inheriting my father's lone wolf nature and having always been making losses since I was small, I have realized I can't live alone.

Please. Anyone. Stay by my side. You can mock me as a sad virgin who is genuinely terrified by such ridiculous fantasies. I’ll take that. Just need me. Just let me love you. I'm begging you. Please don't leave me alone.

Ah, is this 'love?' I finally get it now.

This isn't a joke; I'm serious. So if you feel like it, if it seems like you could let me love you, please reach out. Leave a comment. Tell me I'm not alone and be there to comfort me. Save this miserable, pitiful me.

Sincerely,
