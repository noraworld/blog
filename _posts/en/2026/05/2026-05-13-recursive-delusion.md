## Edge of Despair
In the end, it was all assumptions. This universe, this body, this mind, these feelings. Nothing could be concluded. I thought reaching enlightenment would bring happiness, so I followed Buddhist teachings, only to find myself on the edge of despair, unable to believe in anything. It was just **like feeling an extreme fear, loneliness, and despair as if falling endlessly into a bottomless pit.**

Since everything is an assumption, thinking that enlightenment exists was also an assumption. Just when I thought I was finding some reassurance, I was suddenly plunged into an abyss. I realized that no matter how hard I try, I can't erase fear or anxiety. There was no stage of being free from all suffering.

Delusions and worldly desires can't be erased. Somehow, anger just arises out of nowhere. Even if you think it's gone temporarily, it comes back again. Yet, by realizing it’s a delusion and keeping a distance, there are times when delusions and desires don't arise for a longer period. Continuing this, gradually the sense of having a body also fades away, and the feeling of being here (this body) starts to disappear.

The only thing left is the sensation of breathing. The feeling of air passing through the nostrils. The sensation of the lungs expanding and contracting. If I try to make even that disappear, I can't breathe. Continuing that would probably mean death. So I stop. Then I notice the body feels very light. There's a lightness as if being summoned to heaven. But as it seems like I might actually be summoned to heaven, I get scared and strongly focus on the real world. Returning to daily life, delusions and worldly desires reappear.

The only way to be free from suffering is to die. If there is a state of enlightenment, it’s to die. Letting go of attachments means letting go of even the thought of "wanting to live." Despair.

This kind of despair is just too terrifying. I'm scared to admit it. But if mankind has to die someday, and if dying leads to enlightenment, until I die naturally, I decided to take a break from this despair and return to daily life for a while.

Then, instead of feeling these fears I used to feel, frustration about the unfairness of the world, and losing time for things I love because of work, comes back, just like before. In the end, nothing changed. I wanted to reach enlightenment and stepped into it, but was swallowed by infinite fear, came back because it was too scary, and nothing changed before enlightenment. Distancing from negative thoughts only made other negative thoughts appear.

But at least, thinking that thoughts dominated by feelings of anger are just assumptions, gradually lightens the heart. Thus, even amidst suffering, it gets slightly better. However, considering everything is an assumption, reaching a certain point, a tremendous fear attacks again. The thought of repeating this cycle throughout life made me believe that as long as I’m alive, there’s no way to be free from suffering.

### Wanting to Unify Account Usernames But Can’t, Leading to an Endless Loop
When I didn’t know anything about Eastern philosophy, Buddhism, or Taoism, I experienced something very similar. There was a time I was really fixated on the usernames of my accounts on internet services and wanted to unify them all. However, especially on Twitter (X), the competition is fierce, and I couldn’t get my desired username. Changing all usernames to unify them felt like losing. Moreover, some services don’t allow changing the username once set. In such cases, creating a new account would be the only way to execute, which means parting with all previous data.

I couldn’t do that. It wasn’t just a hassle. It meant separating from all my previous data. What’s more, if I later found myself unhappy with a username I compromised on, I would have to do the same thing again. Then I would never become attached to any account.

But I wanted to be particular about usernames. I wanted uniformity. However, my favorite string of characters was already taken. To change to a compromised name, I’d have to create new accounts. But since it's a compromise, I might want to change it in the future, leading to another creation of new accounts. I’d then never become attached to any account, leading to giving up and continuing to use it as it is, even though I wanted uniformity...

No matter how much I thought, the fact that I couldn't get a username already taken didn’t change, and I got stuck in an infinite loop of obsession. I remember being so frustrated I couldn’t concentrate on work at all.

### Whether It’s Okay to Lose Virginity in a Brothel, Leading to an Endless Loop

Then one day, while chatting with a colleague, the topic of brothels came up. I listened to them talk.

But I can’t...” However, there were brothels! Why was I so particular about it? I should just try it and experience it! Realizing this made me feel refreshed.

But when I thought about actually going, I hesitated. Is it really okay to lose virginity like this? Shouldn't it be with a naturally acquired girlfriend for the memorable first time? Even if the experience wasn’t euphoric, it would become a memory without regret.

But what about a brothel? Ending the important first time in a deliberate, artificial way—is that okay? Isn't it something that should be gained through effort and self-improvement? If I went to a brothel and the person I met wasn’t as expected, I’d regret it even more.

However, sexual desire arises frequently, and every time I take care of it alone, it comes back. This doesn’t satisfy me, and while thinking this, I’d always remain a virgin… (even if I went to a brothel, I’d still be an inexperienced virgin in essence…).

Consistently taking care of sexual desire alone makes it temporarily disappear but continues repeatedly in everyday life. Hence, if I become unable to endure and go to a brothel eventually, isn’t going now the same? Moreover, delaying causes more frustration, leading to suffering; wouldn’t it be better to go sooner?

But that’s a decision when feeling sexual desire, and if it subsides after taking care of it, I’d wonder why I wanted to go to a brothel (or wanted sex). The feeling of having easily ended the first important time would be stronger, leading to regret. However, obsessing about it means I can’t erase the frustrating feelings of dealing with it alone...

In the end, no matter how much I thought, I couldn’t claim either side was wrong, and I got trapped in an endless loop of attachment. It’s just like this 👉 [Buddhism is Sex](https://note.com/noraworld/n/na55cb98ef944)

### Fun and Suffering Continues Forever, Leading to an Endless Loop
All of it was the same as enlightenment. No conclusion was reached no matter how much I thought. Trying to reach enlightenment but couldn't because it didn’t exist.

Well, even if delusions and worldly desires can be eradicated through meditation, they come back soon, meaning it's pointless. Erasing desires and delusions to a great extent might stop sensing them physically and emotionally, but could that be called “living”?

If you feel nothing? Without desires or delusions, there’s no joy, anger, sadness, or fun. Constantly being selfless and unaware of personal actions doesn't result in happiness. The body merely operates mechanically. Can that be called “living”?

This means it's like being an “other.” Without ego or consciousness, merely physically existing in the world. If that’s acceptable, indeed enlightenment exists. Yet that feels no different from being dead. It seems equivalent to living as an “other.”

Consciousness drives the feeling of being alive, and if every action becomes absorbed mindlessly, moments of happiness might not be felt, making it akin to being dead. While not physically dead during sleep, absence of consciousness numbs time perception and actions. Considering it continues throughout awakeness until physical death...

“But you’re conscious while awake,” you might think, but you’ve experienced it; when fully immersed in a game’s excitement, consciousness fades. When truly enjoying something, you forget to even think, “This is fun!”

Of course, after it ends and you regain awareness, recalling past experiences makes you say, “Oh, that was fun!” But that’s imagination or thought process, allowing negative thoughts or imaginations to exist, proving they can’t disappear…

Desires and imaginations drive feelings of enjoyment and displeasure. If desires and imaginations were completely erased, negative things wouldn't be felt, but neither would enjoyable things...making it akin to being dead or living as an “other.” 

Conversely, to experience enjoyment, desires or thoughts are necessary, which simultaneously generates unpleasant feelings...

If the amount of desires and imaginations could be freely controlled, a higher quantity would increase total enjoyment felt, but also unpleasant feelings. Conversely, a lower quantity decreases unpleasant feelings, but also total enjoyment felt...

This mirrors instant death or longevity. Quick death might reduce suffering, but also enjoyment. Longevity brings more enjoyment, yet more suffering. Ultimately, the balance between happiness and suffering remains unchanged.

When happiness or enjoyment are felt in the future, vague anxiety arises, and immense fear ensues. But since it’s baseless fear, realization leads to regaining peace. Then vague anxiety resurfaces, repeated endlessly...

Repeat endlessly? If enlightenment leads to an infinite loop of fear, escaping into this world results in an unending loop of happiness and fear...

Huh? Haven’t I felt similar fear before... Could this be...

## A World Where Happiness and Despair Continue Equally Forever...
Is this reincarnation...? In reincarnation, past life memories aren’t present, right. It’s said suffering in this life leads to less suffering in the next life. Have I experienced this in a previous life? Whether realizing this or not, a life with pleasurable and painful experiences existed... Not only in this life, but it loops forever... Forgetting in the next life, realizing again, or dying unaware, repeating a life of both joy and hardship eternally... Come to think of it, both the concept of reincarnation and Buddhism originated from India, right...?

Am I overthinking...? But as no one knows what lies after death, why dismiss this as overthinking...? Isn’t that just a reassuring attempt to believe it’s not...?

...Something’s wrong. Pondering unknowns endlessly. Yet, it’s just fantasy. Ceasing thought would avoid further fear. But returning to common worldly interpretations means nothing has changed from unenlightenment. If Atman-Brahman Unity is true, it’s possible this real world is an assumed world... Even otherwise (that being an assumption), regardless earthquakes, tsunamis, diseases, and death in this world pose fears...

It’s driving me insane. The same fear as before... Overthinking risks losing sanity. Even if this is purely neural fantasy, worldly fears remain... Eliminating terror would erase joy and pleasure, akin to being dead, equivalent to living as an “other”...

I wish I hadn’t realized this... I shouldn’t have aimed for enlightenment... Is ignorance bliss...? New terrifying realizations keep emerging. Will this really continue forever...?

Assuming this is an assumption and returning to conventional reality interpretations, worldly fears persist, and even earning peace through refuge from these thoughts doesn’t eliminate arising anger, anxiety, and terror. Considering them assumptions, yet physically experiencing earthquakes, tsunamis, illnesses, deaths... Erasing emotions equates death... The difference is narrow or wide interpretation of life: only this life or eternal recurrence... 

Shouting for help, no one comes. Regardless of reassurance or ridicule for these thoughts, proof of dismissal is impossible. Believing terror’s unreasonable deflection, reassurance fosters realized worldly fears and death, yet marks more terror attacking in the future... If not in this life, it might come next... Offering no rest at all... Terrifying, overwhelmingly terrifying...

Science might say such fear-driven thoughts stem from sleep deprivation or caffeine (indeed, I haven’t slept well recently and drank coffee before writing this), but unless ruling out assumptions, an infinite loop traps you...

Stopping these thoughts brings peace again. Yet, choosing comfortable thoughts acknowledges terror, proving it’s erroneous, recognizing fear arises for survival instincts...

Ah, this is too terrifying, losing a sense of physical reality. My body feels hazy. Might continuing this thought call me to heaven...? Even stopping leaves repetition, just without physical death...?

## Thinking About Anything Other Than ‘This Moment’ Is a Waste
But, but, but. No conclusion can be drawn no matter how much this is considered. Could it be thought as follows?

If reincarnation is correct, alternating life’s joy and suffering extends through lives eternally. And resisting this change is impossible, hence surrender is the only option. Negative feelings arise, as do negative experiences, and cannot be avoided.

However, if reincarnation is merely nonsensical, then pondering such fearful notions is a waste. Because denying unending future suffering concludes thinking to live joyfully now is futile. “This life carries endless hardship” may be a delusion, making efficiently joyous living each moment in succession through old age and peaceful death a possibility. This indeed aligns with conventional reality interpretations.

Certainly, anxiety or fear never fully fade, aiming to remove them results in losing the sense of joy, equating to being dead. Furthermore, significant challenges might lie ahead in life. That possibility can’t be dismissed. However, assuming inevitability of such future challenges is also delusional. In the end, both are assumptions, making living joyfully now beyond pondering.

Accepting reincarnation means inevitable eternal cycles (of joy + suffering) exist, hence granting peace by resigning to live now. If this is delusional, believing life as traditionally understood, resisting it as futile, happiness lies in current moments.

### Is the Endless Cycle of Fearful and Reassuring Thought Itself an Assumption?
Often trapped in such infinite loops doesn't imply future thought loops are inevitable assumptions. Falling into thought loops might happen again, yet prior oversight of their ending potential was unacknowledged. That became an overlooked “assumption.”

Given these loop traps repeated recently, a mistaken broader assumption of infinity arose. Encountering future loops implies simply, “Aren’t you still plainly unenlightened?” Ignoring realizing the unexplored possibility! “Enlightenment” is subjective, unclear whether truly achieved. Repeated experiences don’t necessitate endless similar outcomes, that’s the prime assumption!

Meditation or Zen neither promise enlightenment by hours invested, nor routinely offer persistence of a ‘delusion-free’ state. It mirrors physical training for muscles: dedication fulfills, neglect leads to loss.

“The cycle of equal happiness and despair” might be an unfounded assumption. Long-term spiritual discipline makes happiness more tangible, scientifically proven. Viewing it as assumption means the alternative is too, hence either can be believed.

## Insights on Zen Questions and the Middle Way
It feels like tackling a self-created Zen question and unlocking it myself. This clarity after solving a Zen question—is that what it feels like? In NARUTO terms, this is akin to Sasuke and Itachi endlessly looping in genjutsu, breaking free while neither physically moved.

If such belief becomes overbearing, immense fear or heavy disappointment ensue when reality counters belief. Thinking “I’ll die tomorrow!” in true belief is terrifying; truly believing “this universe belongs only to me, with soulless human-acting entities around” crushes with loneliness.

## Thoughts and Emotions Are Like the Weather
Rain never continues forever. It eventually stops. But it doesn’t remain stopped eternally either. It will rain again. There might be times, like the rainy season, when it continues for a while. Times may occur when you worry, “Will it rain forever...?” but in reality, it never does.

You can't increase sunshine or decrease rain, but you can lessen the feeling of “Ugh, this is annoying” when it rains. “Ugh, this is annoying” can't be completely eradicated, and it doesn’t need to be. But it can be reduced or weakened. That's life.
