## This Is the Truth of This Universe!!!
I realized it... Finally, finally, I might have realized it...!!!

**This universe was me!!! The universe itself was the true identity of 'me'!!!**

Until now, I thought the consciousness that is 'me' was inside my brain (which I mistakenly believed was my existence, what is medically called the 'brain' of this 'body'). But that wasn't the case. The universe itself was my consciousness!!!

All the objects in front of my eyes, the scenery, nature, earth, animals, and people—everything that exists in this universe was all mine!!! They were creations of my consciousness!!! (If you look at it from a "relative world", it might feel bad being told "You are my property". I'm sorry. I thought expressing it this way was easy to understand, so forgive me 🙇‍♂️)

That's why everyone in this universe was an irreplaceable part of the universe called 'me'!! You were all precious treasures in the consciousness called 'me'!! Thank you so much, everyone!!!! Thank you so much for being there!!!!! 🙏🙏🙏



## Collected Life's Hidden Threads!
Oh, so that's why. So, by appreciating everyone in this universe that is 'me', there won't ever be anyone threatening me. No, more like there never was to begin with.

But, because I didn't notice that, I'd feel anger when things didn't go my way, or when someone didn't do what I expected. That's like getting mad at a game console because the game isn't going well. That won't make the game go smoothly and even fun things become not fun.

I've been reading Eastern philosophy and Buddhism, and applying those to my life experiences has gradually made Buddhist thoughts clearer. But Upanishadic philosophy (Brahman-Atman unity) and Taoism seemed too far-fetched for me to grasp. "Hmm, is that so" was what I thought.

But both the Brahman-Atman unity (Yajnavalkya), Taoism (Laozi and Zhuangzi), and the Buddhist enlightenment ultimately lead to the same truth. That's why Buddha taught that letting go of attachments liberates you from suffering. Because this universe is me, and everyone in this universe is mine, showing off or striving to achieve meaningless things isn't part of the universe's blueprint!!! That's why it gets tough!

It's like desperately trying to impress a plush toy with wealth display, struggling with things you don't want to do. Earning money itself is great if you enjoy it, but if you earn it just to show off to a plush toy, it's just meaningless. What are you doing, right? Haha.

Besides, if I die, the universe might cease to exist entirely. So why suffer for the future, or even for honor after death, by sacrificing the present? If dying means this universe vanishes, there's no point in doing that!

To go further, maybe the concept of past and future doesn't even exist?? That's why theories like the theory of relativity, where time flow changes with the speed of objects, feel unintuitive and outside of common sense (for those living in a relative world)?

Thinking like this, it's meaningless to cling to the past or the future, isn't it?? **Now!!! This universe only has this moment now!! So living fully in the present moment is all that's needed!!!!!!!**

Because there is no past or future within this consciousness called 'me'!! And since this consciousness 'me' is the entire universe, this universe certainly has no past or future. That's why thinking about the future (worrying if everything will be okay or if I'll have enough money) causes suffering. You're thinking of something non-existent as if it exists. It's like lamenting why some cute ideal girl from a video game isn't here in this world!! Haha.

As I linked at the start, before reaching this point, I read '[Philosophy and Something About Science](https://www.amazon.co.jp/dp/B07K8FHVBX)', learned about quantum mechanics (many-worlds interpretation) and Turing Test (does AI have intelligence or mind?), and suddenly realized. Before realizing it, I had overwhelming fear and loneliness thinking "is it just me in this world (universe)...?".

Ancient people, without any quantum mechanics (many-worlds interpretation), really reached this truth...! The ancient Eastern philosophers are just too amazing...!!



## This Is Not the End. It's Just the Beginning.
Probably, this doesn't mean I've achieved enlightenment. Even tomorrow, I might still get frustrated with others. It's like realizing a game's a game, but still getting frustrated when things don't go well.

However, you play games because they're fun, not for suffering. Surely life is the same. **Life is a game.** **'This body' is the controller to role-play in this vast universe (open world).**

When you play a game, your fingers start moving automatically without your brain consciously thinking, just like 'this body', if not constrained by 'attachments' (like anger towards others), moves naturally. And when you let 'this body' move naturally, you'll get what you truly want. **If you don’t consciously seek something, you’ll get what you really desire.**

When you play a game trying desperately for a perfect clear, by pressing the buttons hard or repeatedly, it doesn't work. But when you think "Oh well, even if I don't clear it perfectly, I guess it's fine" and play purely for enjoyment, the game gives you the best experience. That's what Laozi meant by saying "If you don't cling to who you are, you'll become yourself..."

Sometimes, it might be sad when your save data gets lost. But still, because you love the game, you think, "I'll try hard from the beginning again." That's life. I mean, nobody plays a game fearing they'll lose their save data, right? (If there is, I'm sorry 🙇‍♂️)
