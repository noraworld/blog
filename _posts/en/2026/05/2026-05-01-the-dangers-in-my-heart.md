## The Crazy Stuff in My Heart
When I feel like playing a game again[^game_console] or when I'm watching a famous streamer play games, I start thinking, "I don't get paid for playing games at all, and instead I have to pay for the game console and games. But these famous streamers make millions just by playing games[^video_editing]." It feels pointless to me whether I play or just watch them. And the more I watch, the more I feel like I'm wasting time without gaining any skills, while helping streamers make money, and I think this is not good.

[^game_console]: I don't own a game console. After thinking, "Recording and keeping a record is necessary to make it meaningful," I lost interest and sold my console before moving to Australia.

[^video_editing]: Of course, there's video editing and stuff, but you can always hire someone for that.

No matter how good you are at gaming, talking, or video editing, you can't match the early adopters (those already famous) or cute girl streamers in terms of views or subscribers. Even if you're bad at gaming or talking, you can't beat someone popular and cute in the streaming world.

Men spend money to have sex, while women receive money for having sex. This feels unfair to me. Even if you work hard to get into a prestigious college, graduate, and land a big job, you can't earn more than someone working in the adult industry without special skills or knowledge. I think that's unfair too. And men can't even do the same thing[^male_escort_service], which seems unfair.

[^male_escort_service]: There's something called a female-only adult service, but it's not treated the same as the male one.

In places for meeting the opposite sex like dating apps or matchmaking events, it feels unfair that men have to pay all (or most), make all the effort, plan everything, and then might be blamed if something goes wrong ("You're the one who approached me," they say).

Even if you graduate from college, whether your family is rich or poor determines whether you carry a huge debt like student loans. It's unfair to me that, even if you work hard, you still have to pay taxes and insurance (and the more you earn, the more you pay) and loans.

If you're rich because your app was a hit and you have stable income, it feels unfair that you don't have to work hard to live comfortably.

When I've put so much effort into an amazing article I take pride in, and it gets overshadowed by someone with more fame writing random stuff online, getting more views and likes, it's unfair.

Despite old folks who benefit from young people's hard work and still ridicule us with "Youngsters these days...", we still have to pay taxes and insurance, barely benefiting at all.

When I was a NEET, I realized even if I slack a bit while working, it still pays, thinking it’s better than not working at all.

## Goodbye to Crazy Thoughts 👋
All of it is the crazy stuff in my heart. No matter how convincing the explanation, the world doesn't change. It's pointless to think about it. It only makes me feel bad. Even those who enjoy the sweet perks of life will die someday. And I'm the same. If we all die someday, let’s do what we love until then.

Earning money easily doesn't always mean happiness. Being rich doesn't always mean happiness either.

What’s considered normal, morally or ethically right, isn’t always rational for personal happiness. Even if a thought seems right, not thinking about it might be more rational for happiness. Even if people call you stupid, if you're happy, it's okay.

Whether I try not to contribute any more (***), or just live how I want because nothing matters after we die, it’s up to me.
