## I Started Using Dating Apps Again After a Long Time...
Just a few days ago (on the 25th), I opened a dating app after a long time. As I wrote "after a long time," this isn't my first time using a dating app. I created an account in 2019 and was actively using it at first, but I didn't find any good matches, so I gradually stopped using it.

Since then, I've occasionally thought about trying it again, but it never seems to work out, and I stop using it soon after restarting. Since men generally have to pay, I can't just use it endlessly. Plus, I've become less hopeful each time I try again, thinking it probably won't work out, so even when I become a paid member, it's usually just a one-month plan.

Often, I give up and stop opening the app before the paid subscription expires, and I tend to go a long time without using it again. So even though I started in 2019, I've spent more time not using it.

From the beginning, I've tried four well-known apps (Omiai, Pairs, with, Tapple). Initially, I was a paid member of multiple apps at the same time, but I now know that doing so only costs money. So, I become a paid member only after matching with several people on an app with good reactions (likes and match rates), where you can't exchange messages for free.

The app with the best reactions varies depending on the time, but this time I matched with 3 to 4 people on Omiai (there was no reaction from others), so I became a paid member on Omiai for just one month.

### Going Nowhere...
But as expected, there's no sign of it going well this time either. The first person I matched with liked me first and greeted me cheerfully, but after a few exchanges, their account was already deactivated (blocked?) by the time I opened the app to check.

The second match also didn't respond to my greeting, and just like the first one, their account was deactivated. The second person also liked me first, but it was probably just a mis-tap. Once you like someone, you can't undo it, leading to false hopes.

The third person I matched with turned out to be a foreigner who had to return to their home country soon or someone who didn't reply for a while, and I already feel they are not someone who I could have a meaningful connection with.

### Not Exciting...
Honestly, rather than worrying about whether it will go well or not, I'm just not excited at all... I know it's rude to say, but everyone looks the same to me. The same sort of photos, introductions, and profiles... Swiping through profiles feels like a repetitive task.

I understand. I pursued meeting someone and opened the app after a long time for that reason, but the scene I see is the same repetitive view... I sought this out, but I'm not interested at all... What's that about...?

Sure, I might have indulged in fantasies about fateful encounters, but the gap between imagination and reality is so large that I'm disheartened.

And, to be clear, this isn't just this time. It's something I feel each time I restart, and this time, after a long absence, those empty feelings surfaced again.

Without fear of misunderstanding, and speaking very roughly, if I gave up all conditions I look for in a partner and made having a girlfriend itself the goal, I might be successful (although even thinking this might be arrogant). But obviously, that's not acceptable.

So, am I aiming too high? I personally don't think so. I just have simple wishes, like wanting someone kind, someone who talks to me proactively, someone who likes me. But those can’t be discerned from profiles (though self-proclaimed "kind" people are abundant).

And since the first photo in the profile leaves the most impact, ultimately, it comes down to judging by appearance — cute, good-looking, a nice smile, or seeming cheerful. Unlike the real world, because I can find endless potential partners by swiping, there’s no attachment or affection toward each person.

### Passive...
I don't think everyone is like this, but women are generally passive. So, unless I'm proactively engaging and inviting them out to meals, things won't progress.

And honestly, I’m kind of passive myself. I prefer being wanted rather than doing the wanting. Of course, I can like someone first, but if I sense they don't like me back, I often lose interest. I can't seem to muster the enthusiasm to "make her fall for me no matter what." It feels like I can only truly like someone if they like me first (though if they think the same, our feelings will never align...).

Yet I understand that it should be the same premise (they should be looking for encounters too), but the fact that I have to take the first step demotivates me further. I'm not even excited, and to deepen the relationship, I have to actively invite them to meals, make reservations, set meeting places... When I think about all of that, it feels like too much effort.

I might be hoping for a "wonderful person who will break this pattern," but maybe that's "expecting too much." I end up questioning the point of forcing myself to get a girlfriend... But for some reason, the desire for a partner just won't fade.
