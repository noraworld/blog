When AI first appeared, I resisted it. But now, I feel more at ease when I do things by myself without relying on AI. Take writing this post, for instance. It's a hobby for me. Putting my thoughts into words feels good. So, I write the main content in my own words without using AI (though I use AI for thumbnails and slugs).

Honestly, it’s hard to tell whether a human or AI wrote something. Today’s AI can create text that’s more natural and even better than what many people can write, and it does it instantly.

So, seeking perfection in what humans create doesn’t make much sense anymore. By perfection, I mean things like keeping a consistent style, avoiding typos, and structuring sections properly — all interface-related aspects.

Of course, official documents from companies shouldn't have errors. But even then, AI can handle them since they don’t involve personal thoughts. So, the documents don't need to be perfect when humans write them.

[I've struggled with perfectionism in the past (and still do)](https://note.com/noraworld/n/nde95d094eccb). Writing has been a psychological burden because I thought everything had to be perfect, making me reluctant to write. I often had ideas to write about but didn't, because of the stress of needing perfection. I still feel that way sometimes. It’s a mindset built over many years.

However, considering how AI has evolved, there’s no longer a reason to seek perfection in what I create. AI-created content looks more perfect and gets made instantly. **In fact, now there's more value in writing in my own words, however messy or unpolished it may be, because it's more human.**

Approaching it this way, seeking perfection seems to lessen the value of what I create. AI's advancement helps ease my perfectionism, along with the general benefits of better efficiency and productivity.

The same goes for coding. In the past, I needed to personally write and implement all the code for projects I wanted to create. If I was going to release them, I felt they had to be long-lasting, maintainable, and polished, even if they were just for fun.

As a result, despite having many ideas, I often couldn’t start them due to this mindset. I lost motivation, or changes over time made some projects unfeasible before I could even begin them.

But now, we have AI agents. [When they first came out, I resisted because they easily created things I spent a lot of effort on. I thought I couldn’t accept such things as my products](https://noraworld.github.io/blog/codex-first-impressions).

Now, I don’t have to code myself because AI agents can do it for me. I just need to come up with ideas and specifications, which is the most enjoyable part for me. Even if there are bugs or changes needed, AI can fix them quickly. It means I don’t have to create perfect, maintainable things from the start. I can ask for help and release things more casually. This is a direct and mental benefit from AI.

AI is a current paradigm shift, so many people feel resistance, similar to switching from paper to digital. Even I, who make a living from technology (programming), felt resistance to AI (specifically AI agents) at first. I wondered what all my efforts were for if AI is much faster, even more than any super engineer, especially for coding. I questioned my own purpose.

Recently, I've realized how much mindset can change my approach and mental well-being.
