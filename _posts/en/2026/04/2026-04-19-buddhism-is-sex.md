## Blasphemy? No!
Why can I state the title of this article without fear……

It's because anyone who says, "Buddha would be mad at you for saying such a thing!" or "It's blasphemous to say that about a sacred religion!" doesn't understand Buddhism at all. I can boldly say it because I fully understand it. **Buddhism is like sex**.



## Do You Really 'Know' Sex?
Do you truly know what the act of "sex" is?

You might think, "Everyone knows that, I'm not a kid."

But there could be people who say:

"I don't know what sex is because I'm a virgin."

If you think that way and haven't yet attained enlightenment, it may not be so hard for you to achieve it. Because that's the essence of Buddhism (Eastern philosophy).

Logically speaking, it means this:

Any adult knows what the act of sex is through words (analytical knowledge). But there are adults who have never actually had sex. This means some people have not experienced it firsthand (intuitive knowledge).

If we define "knowing" as understanding through words, then there are likely no adults who "don't know" sex. But if we define it as knowing through actual experience, then there are indeed adults who "don't know" sex (I'm one of them).

The latter, knowing through experience, is called "realization" in Japanese, but that doesn't really matter. Words are just words, and no matter how much you understand with words, there's an essence you can never grasp until you experience it.

Anyone who has had sex will understand this. No matter how much you learn about what sex is like, or how to enhance pleasure, you can't truly understand the pleasure of sex until you experience it.



## Organizations for Experiential Understanding
The same goes for Buddhism (Eastern philosophy). You can find all sorts of information about Eastern philosophy and Buddhism in online articles and books. But no matter how much you understand and gain knowledge of what "achieving enlightenment" means through words (analytical knowledge), you can't achieve enlightenment unless you have a strong experiential understanding (intuitive knowledge) that makes you go, "Ah! Is that what life's sufferings are all about?"

And because that's the case, it's impossible to express this in words for everyone to understand. Buddhism was created to achieve an experiential understanding, and sometimes it intentionally says unscientific things, lies, or contradictions (lies as expedients).

However, if you judge it as merely a religion or something unscientific and occult-like, you won't truly understand Buddhism. Conversely, just because you become a devout believer in what's said in Buddhism doesn't mean you'll understand the "truth."

In fact, if you can understand the truth, you don't need to convert, follow the precepts, or worship Buddha (it's said that Buddha actually denied such mystical elements).



## How Can I Achieve Enlightenment? (Or Have Sex?)
The pleasure from sex (enlightenment) can't be gained without actually having sex. So Buddhism was about teaching Buddha's disciples "how to have sex," and that was recorded in scriptures and books.

And of course, "how to have sex" will differ from person to person. Biologically, or rather by definition (assuming we don't call doing it alone or imagining it "sex"), you can't start without a partner. In that sense, there's likely no disagreement that finding a partner is the first step.

So, how do you find a partner? Telling someone with no opposite-sex colleagues, "Office romance is great!" is meaningless. Telling someone who doesn't have a smartphone or doesn't know how to use one (though it's unclear how many such people exist today) "Use a dating app!" won't help them take action.

That's why Buddha taught each person how to achieve enlightenment in a way that suited them. In the previous examples, maybe someone was recommended office romance, and maybe someone was encouraged to use dating apps.

If you interpret the "this is how you should do it" part as a universal "fact," contradictions will naturally arise. But that's not the essence. If "that person" in front of him could achieve the goal, then that was OK for Buddha.

⚠️ Since I used the strong word "sex" and explained it as needing a partner first, let me clarify to avoid misunderstandings: I'm not advocating office romance or using dating apps solely for the purpose of having sex (doing it just for that reason).



## Experiential Understanding = Buddhism = Sex
To fully understand what life's sufferings are through intense experiences (realization). That's what "achieving enlightenment" means, and it's the path Buddha (Buddhism) aimed for.

Eastern philosophy, Buddhism, achieving enlightenment, knowing the ultimate truth are essentially about this. In that sense, Buddhism is like sex.

In this context, it doesn't have to be sex. But sex is an experience that even some adults haven't had, making it the most relatable, universal, and intense example. For travel enthusiasts, the following might resonate more.

You can't understand the grandeur of Machu Picchu without visiting it. Just seeing photos or videos and acquiring knowledge doesn't mean you've truly "known" its grandeur. Travel enthusiasts would find this obvious. Achieving enlightenment (being liberated from life's sufferings, understanding the universe's truth) is akin to this.
