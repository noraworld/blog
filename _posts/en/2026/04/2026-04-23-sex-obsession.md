Even though I've been writing so much about letting go of attachments, maybe I haven't let go of my attachment to sex yet. Talking about it is like asking, "What about you?"



## Little by Little
I've already mentioned in a few articles, and it's no longer a big revelation, but I'm a virgin. I'm 30 now, turning 31 this year, and I haven't had sex or even had a proper girlfriend. In today's world (especially in Japan), that's not so rare, so when I reveal it, most people just think, "So what?" or "Oh, that's sad (LOL)."

But considering most people have already "graduated," it's natural to feel sex is something special.

Of course, from an Eastern philosophy (Buddhist) standpoint, that's just an illusion. A sex worker's view of sex isn't the same as a virgin's. Just like someone who lived overseas due to their parents' circumstances doesn't feel the same about overseas trips as someone going abroad for the first time as an adult.



## Feeling Conflicted
I even think so when seeing amateur women in adult videos (whether they’re truly amateurs or not is another story). Not all of them are doing it professionally (though some might). Some might be doing it not out of interest in the act itself but for other reasons.

So, often you see women who seem unhappy or unwilling. Even though the older man tries to engage genuinely, the responses are off, and there’s a lack of enthusiasm during the act.

Maybe they're okay with it being like that, but if that's the sex I've been dreaming of, can it really be satisfying...?

On the other hand, there are videos where hidden cameramen aren't present, so they’re too worried about whether they’re capturing it right, constantly glancing at the camera from behind the mask. It’s just so mindless and unfocused.

Of course, some aspects focus more on making videos than the sexual act itself, so it's not fair to generalize, but I hear that some people meet the opposite sex just for the purpose of such acts (so-called "hookups"). Even when that's consensual and legal, I can’t help but wonder if it truly satisfies the heart.

Maybe your body feels better. But what about your heart? Even when instincts are satisfied, does an emptiness linger...?



## No Way
Since I've never experienced any kind of sex, it's entirely imagined, but seeing some sex that doesn’t seem satisfying objectively suggests that this special feeling about sex is just a delusion.

Actually wanting to have sex merely as an act while not having someone I love seems to show an attachment.

A friend once told me about his first experience at a brothel (not the first time in that sense). Though it felt satisfying, he didn't feel fulfilled in his heart, so while it was a good experience, he wouldn't go again. Others who quit late shared similar insights: “When I actually did it, it was like, ‘Oh, that’s it?’ Just unimpressive.”



## Agonizing
Having never experienced it, I might be assuming it’ll be an ecstatic, incredible feeling, but going to a place just for the experience might end up with me going, “Oh, that’s it...” and being let down.

Because it's not with someone I truly love, I might end up regretting losing my virginity that way. I might feel empty for paying such a high service fee with nothing to show for it.

But if I’m truly not attached, then I shouldn’t agonize over this and just graduate at a place. “Oh, so that’s what it’s like,” and move on. That way, I won’t keep wondering, “What’s it like? Does it feel good? Am I nervous?” and I’ll be (in both senses) freed of the stress.



## Hesitating
So why am I still hesitating to go? Maybe it’s because of the attachment to having my first time with someone I truly care about, or the fear of falling for an amazing service there, or maybe the attachment to money and fairness isn’t totally gone yet.



## Well
No matter how you look at it, you might fail your first time with someone you love due to nervousness, which would make it a traumatic experience. But even if you do, it would make a good laugh and a popular blog story, and logically, there's no right or wrong.

Life has its ups and downs. Go with the flow. Whether or not to graduate at a place doesn't matter. Go if you want to, or don't. Let things happen. Even if I end life as a virgin, who cares?
