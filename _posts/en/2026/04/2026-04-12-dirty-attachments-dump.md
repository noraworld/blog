### Obsession With Unfairness
For me, the strongest obsession was with records. Why "records"? Because I felt intense anger at the unfairness in the world.

Both my friend and I graduated college, but since my friend's family was wealthy, their parents paid for their education, leaving them debt-free (no student loans). Meanwhile, my family wasn't well-off, so I still have student loan debt. The total amount before I started repayment was 4.5 million yen (still paying it off).

Even though we just graduated college, one starts their professional life without debt because of the family they were born into, while another starts with a 4.5 million yen debt. Yet there's no extra social advantage, as we pay taxes and insurance fees like those without debt. I thought, how could such unfairness exist?

### Obsession With Labor
Also, working was painful for me. Not necessarily because the work content was painful (though that happened sometimes), but because labor is essentially something you do for living expenses and money.

However, that living expense money gets taken away by debts, taxes, and insurance premiums. So what is the point of working hard to raise your salary?—I wondered.

Since graduating college, I've worked as an IT engineer (programmer). I don't particularly dislike it, but I dream of creating my original content and sharing it with everyone for a living. I have this desire to own and share the content I create myself. "Original content" can take any form, and even this article I am writing now is one such example. I want to share my thoughts, ideas, and perspectives.

However, when employed by a company (whether as a full-time employee or contractor), you can earn money as compensation. But all the work you create belongs to the company. "This part of the program is mine!"—Legally, you can't make that claim. This contradicts my desire to "own and share content I create."

Instead of freely expanding my ideas and making them happen, I create what the company asks me to. Of course, there is room for creativity in the algorithms or coding style of the programs (which can be enjoyable), but the finished product (the working program) isn't a form of my idea, nor can I claim it's "mine."

Consuming time and labor and converting it to money. But since money is just numbers (indistinguishable by appearance), you can't tell whether it's hard-earned or slyly acquired. Even if it's hard-earned, it goes towards repaying loans (student loans), taxes, and insurance. The deductions also vary based on the wealth of your birthplace family within the same country and differ by country laws even when earning similarly... Such futility, I thought.

To say more, no matter how skilled I become or how earnestly I work, probably a hostess earns more than I do monetarily. But for me back then, work was only "a means to an end," so I only wanted to earn the minimum required in the shortest possible time and increase my leisure time. 

Don't get me wrong. I have no prejudice against the sex industry and understand it is a very taxing job. But, no matter what efforts I made or how much I wanted to do that job, I couldn't become a hostess. I wasn't jealous; my obsession was with the inequality.

And, if only considering the viewpoint of a "means to an end," the job of a hostess (ignoring difficulty for now) seemed attractive to me. You can earn what you need for living expenses in a short time and don't have to work 5 days a week like many workers. In my rage of wanting to but not being able to, I was irrationally jealous of hostesses I hadn't even met (of course, knowing my obsessions now, I no longer wish to become a hostess).

I digress a bit, but simply put, even if I genuinely enjoyed my work, I felt the need to ignore it and thought, "Since I'm being exploited in labor, I should quickly finish and make it my own time." Finishing work early in itself may not be bad, but I constantly suffered from the idea, "I'm being exploited."

### Obsession With Time
After work ends or on holidays, I must commit to content creation to escape employed work as soon as possible. I was driven by this strong sense of urgency. Yet, being tired from work or sleep-deprived, I found myself very irritated, too drained to work on content creation.

The desire to create content persists even now, having let go of that obsession. But the intense obsession to dedicate as much time as possible to break free from employed labor left me stressed, unable to perform as I wanted. It was a hellish time where what I wanted to do became distressing to pursue.

### Obsession With Record Keeping
So, what about the "obsession with record keeping" mentioned at the beginning? I couldn't shake these obsessions (money, unfairness, work, free time) until I reached enlightenment, so I tried to record everything. Essentially, I wanted to pass on my tormented life story to future generations, leave proof of such a tragic life via records.

To achieve this, I was obsessed with recording everything happening around me, thoughts crossing my mind, and future actions I intended to take. I spent much of my everyday life recording.

Spent hours writing daily journals, noting how much time I spent reading and my thoughts on it, writing scathing notes about annoying people, recording my sleep hours, meals of the day, the volume of urine, the weight of feces (sorry for the yucky details 🙇‍♂️)... Even more absurdly, questioning if I should also record cutting nails. I even tried to expand the list.

When using dating apps, I recorded all conversation logs, thinking how unfair it was that men had to pay exorbitantly while getting cursory replies or being ignored, rarely even matching (another obsession).

Straight up, it's creepy and stalker-like. "That's why you're still a virgin!"—I can say now, but back when I was suffering, I couldn't accept that fact. Plus, I thought going to a brothel was "playing into their hands," so I couldn't become even an inexperienced virgin, pathetic as I was.

Moreover, I felt obligated to do this and that I'd lose life's proof if I slacked. To a greater extent, I felt I had to preserve these records even after death, which led to strong security settings to avoid hacking, reluctance about app permissions, avoiding services likely to close (guessing unsoundly), and operating with minimal cost (maintenance costs disappear at death). This obsessive chain burdened me. It was overwhelming, feeling I couldn't stay grounded. Not relying on family or anyone, desperately wanting to leave something behind even if it felt as impossible as avoiding death...

I still continue these records. This writing, too, is a record. But now, it's not out of obligation but from wanting to. Hence, there's no "obsession" in it. **The problem isn't in recording but in having an obsession.**

### Other Obsessions
There were times when I couldn't even live properly due to a lack of money, and repeated tax and insurance reminders came. Credit card payments defaulted, switching automatically to high-interest revolving payments, leading to more debt, spawning hatred for the state and the financial industry.

Despite having student loans, living expenses were unmanageable; taxes and insurance were demanded. It felt meaningless to work under such burdens; clearly, surviving felt pointless...

I had other obsessions, too, but going further would make this too much "personal talk," stopping here. (There's also dissatisfaction with family; it's been years since seeing or contacting them, but that's another whole article worth—so omitting here.)

I made an "Obsession List" for myself, and decided to leak it online as well. Feel free to check it out, though I won't be responsible for any discomfort 👉 [Obsession List](https://noraworld.github.io/blog/obsessions)

### Nastier Than Poop
Reflecting on my obsessions, I thought, "Wow, this is nastier than poop 💩." Posting such a piece on the internet is like defecating in public (apologies for the crude expression 🙇‍♂️).

Needless to say, when struggling with these obsessions, I couldn't possibly share such an article publicly. If someone who irritated me read it, they might belittle me, provoking a rage that would drive me to ***...

Yet realizing it all stemmed from obsession and delusion, I wondered, "Why resist publicly defecating?" I forgot how to feel shame, worry, or suffer. I want to know defeat now.

Of course, literally doing it is criminal most of the time and meaningless for me. But sharing such shame equivalent to public defecation could help others currently suffering, hence writing this article. I didn't "endure the shame" but "forgot the shame" to write it."

Everyone's obsessions differ, so merely reading this won't instantly resolve your problems, but I wanted to convey that "obsession"—be its reason. Even someone like me with "obsessions poopified" broke free.

If you find your obsession, life becomes easy. No, **that’s where real life begins.** Why can't it be you, too? “If it can’t be done, then rebut it, hototogisu.”
