### Introduction
Yesterday, I posted an article titled [So, Let's Buy an Apple Watch: Replacing My Apple Watch After 7 Years](https://note.com/noraworld/n/n31fd4f21b21e), so the sequence got a bit mixed up, but this article was actually supposed to go first. If you read my past article titled [Reasons Why I Haven't Used Note Actively and What I Want the Management to Do](https://note.com/noraworld/n/nfd9854191998), you'll see that I've not been using Note actively. Even though the official setting doesn't show when I created the account, looking at the generated date of the password in 1Password, it was February 21, 2020. So more than six years have passed, but including this one, I've only posted six times (the Apple Watch article was the fifth post).

### The Appeal of Note
However, I couldn't forget about Note (it sounds like talking about an ex-girlfriend). Unlike personal blogs, which have limited SEO optimization, Note often appears higher in search results, making it more likely to be read. Plus, there's a community within Note itself. Meaning, your articles can be read by others through related articles and hashtags, and if it gets popular, it might even appear at the top of Note. Participating in the regular topics that Note's official team hosts could also be fun.

I feel like Note is filled with motivation to post articles. Since I'm spending time writing, I want those who are looking for it to read it. Seeing how other people's Note articles and topics can inspire me to write something, I realize that the excitement of possibly getting unexpected feedback is something you can't really experience without a community-based platform like Note.

And, though I'll be writing more about it in each article, I've been feeling particularly dissatisfied with the world and stuck in life lately. When I feel that frustration, I often want to write things down, so I wanted a place to release that stress as well. Though personal blogs rarely get read, carrying all that frustration and blockage and venting it just feels a bit empty.

So, the desire to use Note as an outlet for those emotions has been growing stronger by the day. I actually like writing (even though I'm not good at it), so even without these reasons, I've always wanted to express my thoughts and feelings in writing.

### The Problem
However, just as I wrote in [Reasons Why I Haven't Used Note Actively and What I Want the Management to Do](https://note.com/noraworld/n/nfd9854191998), the works I create (not just writing) are like my children. Therefore, I want to keep a complete copy on hand without relying on a specific platform.

While you can download what you post to Note using the export feature, it comes in XML format. This format is clearly intended for importing into other platforms, rather than keeping as a record on hand. This is one of my discontents with not being able to keep my data the way I want when relying on a specific platform (not just Note).

That's why, when it comes to preserving data, I prefer something like GitHub Pages where the content you have is the master data and it's published on the internet. Also, since it's based on Git, you can download everything in the format you intend with just one command, which is optimal for data preservation. Simply put, I want to keep my blog article data in Markdown, but it's hard to do if you write directly on Note.

### Solution
But manually posting to both GitHub and Note is a hassle. I wanted to solve that somehow. At the end of February, I finally got around to addressing that issue (for now). I won't go into details here, but if you randomly check GitHub or so, you'll know what I mean. If anyone from the management sees this, please understand that I have no intention of misuse 🙏🙏🙏

I stress that it's the people who misuse the tools who are at fault, not the tools themselves or the creators. In such topics, the analogy of a kitchen knife is often used. In a murder case using a knife as a weapon, it's not the craftsman's fault for making a tool that can kill people, which should go without saying. However, in Japan, it's disappointing that those issues tend to arise. The Winny incident is a prime example. We must never forget the outrageous actions of the Kyoto Police at that time, which were absolutely unforgivable.

### Conclusion
Anyway, having posted [So, Let's Buy an Apple Watch: Replacing My Apple Watch After 7 Years](https://note.com/noraworld/n/n31fd4f21b21e), I confirmed there aren't major issues, so I want to use Note more actively from now on. I do enjoy expressing my thoughts and feelings in writing after all. As I mentioned earlier, there are many topics I want to write about, like work, money, and the state of the world. Most of them are filled with dissatisfaction, but even those negative emotions can be channeled into creative output as a driving force to possibly alleviate some of that discontent.

This article got mixed up with the Apple Watch post, so I wasn't really feeling it. Maybe because I can't write in detail here, or maybe what I want to say overlaps with [Reasons Why I Haven't Used Note Actively and What I Want the Management to Do](https://note.com/noraworld/n/nfd9854191998), or maybe because the issue has been somewhat resolved in my mind. Either way, just stating simply that I want to use Note seriously might suffice. Nonetheless, I want to update more casually based on my moods rather than forcing a formal tone, which often makes it painful and less enjoyable to write.

In the future, I would love to make a living through personal activities like writing. I'll write more in detail about this in a separate article about dissatisfaction with work (labor). But clearly, working for a company or being employed doesn't suit me. I'd prefer a way of living where I can subsist through my creative works. Although making a living that way is a challenging path reserved for a select few, leading to significant life instability, I'd rather pursue a life where I engage in activities I'm passionate about than end my life exhausted and despairing in a job that doesn't suit me.

Note also has a monetization system. I forgot to mention this, but it’s another reason I want to seriously use Note. I don't know how far I can go without writing skills or recognition, but I want to give it a shot. I currently have no plans to make paid articles, but if I keep up with Note and start getting a reasonable number of views, I want to consider monetization and gradually shift to this kind of personal creative work.
