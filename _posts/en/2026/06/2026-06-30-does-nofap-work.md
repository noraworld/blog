Yet activities like watching pornography or masturbating release dopamine to satisfy sexual desire, but they don't accomplish reproduction. This unmet expectation might drive a craving for greater stimulation, leading to an addiction to dopamine through repeated porn use or masturbation.

By restoring normal dopamine levels and functions, proponents argue, people may regain interest in things they previously ignored, allocate preserved dopamine to other activities, and wake up more refreshed thanks to surplus dopamine.

Even those against abstinence might agree on this principle, but they might question if restraint must extend to all pleasure sources. This contentious point divides even the scientific community. Some studies report that ejaculation decreases testosterone, while others claim it increases. Critics often point to research suggesting that abstaining raises testosterone during the first week but shows no change afterward.

This issue seems unresolved, and responses may vary among individuals, affected by individual health and conditions.
