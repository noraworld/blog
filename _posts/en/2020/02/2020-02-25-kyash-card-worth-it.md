Today, I got an email from Kyash announcing that you can now apply for the Kyash Card.

[Kyash Card](https://kyash.co/kyashcard/)

As someone who uses about 10 different credit cards, including the Kyash Card Lite (formerly known as the light blue Kyash real card), I'm curious about whether this card is a good deal or not.

In short, from a cashback perspective, I don't think the Kyash Card is that great compared to other credit cards.

The main reason is that I currently use a credit card with an effective cashback of 1.45%. So, simply put, the card I already have is a better deal than the Kyash Card with its 1.0% cashback rate.

With the Kyash Card Lite, since it involves payment from the Kyash balance, you could still collect points from both the card you use to charge the balance and the Kyash Card itself. Even after the cashback rate dropped from 2% to 1%, you can still enjoy double points and a high cashback rate (though it will drop further to 0.5% starting April 2020...).

I haven't done extensive research on the Kyash Card, so I might be wrong, but I don't think you can get double points with the Kyash Card.

The Kyash Card Lite was more like a debit card rather than a credit card because it involved payment from the balance. It was a unique card you could get without a credit check.

However, with the new Kyash Card requiring a credit check, it seems to function more like a regular credit card, allowing you to make payments with just the Kyash Card. So, you probably can't get double points.

Assuming you can't get double points, considering the cashback is 1.0%, if you're only looking to gain from cashback rates, then it might not be necessary to apply for this card.

Of course, credit cards are about more than just cashback rates. The Kyash Card does have its own attractive features. For example, you can easily change the spending limit in the app, and view transaction history, which is a great feature in keeping with the times.

However, since my main reason for using multiple credit cards is to get the best deals (meaning the highest cashback rates) at various services and stores, for those who prioritize cashback rates, the new Kyash Card might not have that much to offer.

This discussion is based on the assumption that you can't get double points. If you can get double points with the Kyash Card, then it would certainly be worth applying for in terms of cashback, considering that Kyash Card Lite will drop to 0.5% cashback in April 2020.

The Kyash Card is still new, so there's not a lot of information available. As more information comes out and if benefits become apparent, it might be worth considering applying.

## Extra Note 1

This is just my guess, but I don't think Kyash is promoting the cashback rate with the Kyash Card.

The app is convenient to use, and you can temporarily stop the card with just one button in the app, which provides peace of mind in the event of theft, suggesting that they are promoting convenience and security.

Still, I think one reason people expect high cashback rates from the Kyash Card is due to the influence of the Kyash Card Lite. Starting with a 2% cashback rate and the ability to earn double points made it a standout card, so it's not surprising that people, including me, expect the same from its successor, the Kyash Card...

## Extra Note 2

The name "Kyash Card" is fine in writing, but explaining it to people gets tricky. It sounds just like "cash card" (the one you use to withdraw money), which is confusing... I usually end up saying something like "K's cash card" or "K-cash card."
